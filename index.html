Bio

My goal as a doctoral researcher is to understand the production and consumption of misinformation in the Global South, with the ultimate goal of creating effective content moderation and platform designs that incorporate systems that promote news literacy for all users.

I am currently a Researcher at St. Francis Institute of Technology, affiliated with the University of Mumbai. My work focuses on areas such as AI-driven document enhancement, applicant tracking systems, and forensic analysis of fake images using machine learning techniques. Before joining St. Francis Institute of Technology, I completed my Master’s in Information Systems from Syracuse University, where I specialized in Data Science. During my graduate studies, I worked as a Research Analyst at Syracuse University, exploring topics like digital transformation and AI in social media content moderation. I also hold a Bachelor’s degree in Computer Engineering from the University of Mumbai. Prior to my academic pursuits, I gained extensive industry experience through internships and contracts with organizations like The Washington Post, where I improved data pipelines and campaign performance, and Revere VC, where I managed financial data using advanced AI OCR algorithms. Additionally, I have collaborated on projects leveraging cloud technologies, including building Azure and AWS-based data pipelines. Throughout my academic and professional journey, I’ve contributed to impactful projects and research papers on topics like malaria parasite detection using machine learning and performance classification using advanced statistical approaches.
